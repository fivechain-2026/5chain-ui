The problem

Crores of stock.
Zero credit.

India's warehouse operators sit on billions of rupees of inventory they can't borrow against. Not because the stock isn't there — but because lenders can't verify it.

01
Records that can't be trusted
Paper stock registers, WhatsApp photos, and Excel sheets are falsifiable. Lenders know it — so they discount or reject.
02
Verification takes weeks
Physical audits cost ₹50,000+ and take 2–3 weeks. By the time credit is approved, the working capital crisis has passed.
03
No standard proof format
Every NBFC asks for different documents. There is no universal, lender-accepted format for warehouse inventory proof.

Platform features

Everything a lender
needs to say yes

🔗
Immutable audit log
Every stock event is hash-chained to the previous one. Any tampering breaks the chain instantly — detectable by any party. 30 days of continuous history builds undeniable credibility.
Core trust layer
₹
FIFO stock valuation
Batch-level FIFO and WAC valuation with automatic LTV application per SKU category. Agri gets 75% LTV. FMCG gets 70%. Packaging is excluded. Every number is auditable to its source GRN.
Finance layer
📋
Goods receipt notes (GRN)
Every inbound shipment creates a QC-verified GRN before stock is credited. Supplier, vehicle, PO reference, and inspector sign-off are all captured and locked.
Inbound module
🏢
Multi-tenant isolation
Multiple suppliers can store goods in the same warehouse. Each sees only their own inventory. Isolation is enforced at the database level via Row Level Security — not just the UI.
Architecture
🔑
Token-gated lender access
Share a time-limited, read-only link with any NBFC or lender. No account required. Links expire automatically. Revocable in one click. Lenders see exactly what you choose to share.
Lender layer
📡
Blockchain settlement (Phase 4)
Hyperledger Fabric L1 settlement with ZK-Rollup scaling. Mathematical proof of inventory state for multi-party trust — without revealing competitor data. The moat that takes 18 months to build.
